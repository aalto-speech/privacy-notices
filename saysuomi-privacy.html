SaySuomi – Privacy Policy

Last updated: 12 March 2026

Privacy Policy

Aalto ASR research group built the SaySuomi app as a Free app. This SERVICE is provided by the Aalto ASR research group at no cost and is intended for use as is.

This page is used to inform visitors regarding our policies with the collection, use, and disclosure of Personal Information if anyone decides to use our Service.

If you choose to use our Service, then you agree to the collection and use of information in relation to this policy. The Personal Information that we collect is used for providing and improving the Service. We will not use or share your information with anyone except as described in this Privacy Policy.

The terms used in this Privacy Policy have the same meanings as in our Terms and Conditions, which are accessible at SaySuomi unless otherwise defined in this Privacy Policy.

What we collect

We only access your microphone when you actively use a speaking function in the app. We do not collect your name, email address, phone number, or other direct contact details through the app for the automated speaking assessment (ASA) research function.

A. If you use the pronunciation practice function

  • Voice recording – the audio you record for pronunciation practice.
  • Practice text – the text you type or select for the exercise.

These data are processed to generate immediate feedback for you. The audio recording from pronunciation practice is not stored after the feedback has been returned, and pronunciation practice sessions are not added to the ASA research dataset.

B. If you use the automated speaking assessment (ASA) function

When you use the ASA function, we collect and store data related to that function for scientific research and system development.

  • Pseudonymous user identifier – a randomly generated identifier used to link your ASA sessions without storing your name or contact details.
  • Participation / consent record – a record that you were shown the relevant information and that you chose to participate in the ASA research component, including the time of your choice and the version of the notice or consent text shown.
  • Audio recordings – the speech recordings you submit when completing ASA speaking tasks.
  • Task content and responses – the speaking prompt, question, or practice text and your response to it.
  • Automated test performance – the scores, ratings, feedback, and other outputs generated by the automatic speech recognition and automated speaking assessment systems from your response.
  • Historical performance data – past and current ASA results associated with your pseudonymous identifier so that progress over time can be studied and, where applicable, shown to you in the app.
  • Timestamps and usage records – when the ASA function is used and which ASA task was completed.
  • Background information – educational background, language background, Finnish proficiency level, language learning behaviour, gender, and first language.
  • Self-assessment – your own assessment of your language skills or of your performance in a speaking task.
  • User opinions and feedback – your opinions about the speaking task, the automated speaking assessment results, and the feedback provided by the app, including brief in-app reactions and any comments if such input is available.

What we do and do not store

  • Pronunciation practice – recordings are processed to provide feedback and are not stored after the result has been returned.
  • Automated speaking assessment (ASA) – recordings and related ASA data are stored as part of the research dataset and system-development data.

Purpose of processing

We process personal data for the following purposes:

  • To provide the speaking functions of the app and generate automated feedback.
  • To provide the automated speaking assessment function and, where applicable, show users their past and current ASA results.
  • To develop, train, improve, and evaluate automatic speech recognition (ASR), automated speaking assessment (ASA), and automated feedback for L2 Finnish.
  • To conduct scientific research on second-language speech, speaking assessment, and speech technology.
  • To analyse users’ background information, self-assessments, and opinions on speaking tasks, automated assessment results, and feedback in order to improve the app and the research methods.

We do not use the data for advertising, commercial profiling, or unrelated commercial purposes.

Legal basis

The legal basis for processing data collected through the automated speaking assessment function is the performance of a task carried out in the public interest, namely scientific research, in accordance with Article 6(1)(e) of the GDPR and the Finnish Data Protection Act.

We also record your consent choice regarding participation in the automated speaking assessment research function. Where we ask for additional optional permissions, we rely on your consent under Article 6(1)(a) GDPR. You may withdraw such consent at any time without affecting the lawfulness of processing carried out before withdrawal.

Who can access the data

Access to stored ASA research data is limited to authorised members of the Aalto research team and to persons who need access for research-related tasks such as transcription, analysis, or technical maintenance under appropriate confidentiality and access controls.

Where applicable, trusted service providers acting on behalf of Aalto University may process data for hosting, storage, or technical support.

Transfers outside the EEA

Personal data collected through the app for the ASA research function is processed within the European Economic Area (EEA). We do not transfer this data outside the EEA.

How long we keep the data

  • Pronunciation practice recordings are not stored after feedback has been returned.
  • ASA research data is stored for the duration of the research and for five years after the end of the project in order to complete research-related publications and verification of results. The data will be deleted from the universities’ storage solutions in 2031.

Security

We protect personal data with appropriate technical and organisational safeguards. Access to stored ASA data is restricted to authorised persons who need the data for research purposes. Security measures may include user authentication, access control, logging, encryption, and secure storage environments.

Because voice data can be inherently identifying, research data is handled using pseudonymous identifiers instead of direct identifiers wherever possible.

Automated decision-making

The app uses automated speech recognition and automated speaking assessment models to generate scores and feedback. These outputs are intended to support practice, learning, and research. They are not used to make decisions that produce legal effects or similarly significant effects on you.

Children’s privacy

The automated speaking assessment research function is intended for adult language learners. We do not knowingly collect ASA research data from children. If you believe that a child has provided personal data to us through the ASA function, please contact us so that we can review the situation.

Your rights

Under the GDPR, you may have the right to:

  • know whether your personal data is being processed,
  • access your personal data,
  • request correction of inaccurate data,
  • request deletion of your data,
  • request restriction of processing,
  • object to processing,
  • withdraw consent where processing is based on consent, and
  • lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority.

Because ASA data is stored under a pseudonymous identifier rather than your name, you will generally need to provide that identifier when exercising your rights. Please note that some rights may be limited where data is processed for scientific research and where full deletion or alteration would seriously impair the research.

You also have the right to lodge a complaint with the Office of the Data Protection Ombudsman in Finland.

Changes to this Privacy Notice

We may update this Privacy Notice from time to time. If we make changes, we will post the updated version here and update the “Last updated” date above.
