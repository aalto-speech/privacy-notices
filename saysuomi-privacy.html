SaySuomi – Privacy Policy

Last updated: 12 March 2026

Privacy Policy

Aalto ASR research group built the SaySuomi app as a Free app. This SERVICE is provided by the Aalto ASR research group at no cost and is intended for use as is.

This page is used to inform visitors regarding our policies with the collection, use, and disclosure of Personal Information if anyone decides to use our Service.

If you choose to use our Service, then you agree to the collection and use of information in relation to this policy. The Personal Information that we collect is used for providing and improving the Service. We will not use or share your information with anyone except as described in this Privacy Policy.

The terms used in this Privacy Policy have the same meanings as in our Terms and Conditions, which are accessible at SaySuomi unless otherwise defined in this Privacy Policy.

What we collect

We get information about you in a range of ways. The App has two distinct functions that handle voice recordings differently, as described below.

Information You Give Us. We collect information you directly give us, including:

  • Voice recordings – audio recordings captured when you use either the pronunciation practice function or the automated speaking assessment function of the App. We only record audio when you actively engage with one of these functions – we do not access the microphone of your device at any other time.
  • Practice text – the Finnish text you type or select as your speaking prompt.
  • Background information (optional) – background metadata you choose to provide, such as educational background, language background, Finnish proficiency level, language learning behaviour, gender, and first language. Providing this information is voluntary and is used solely for research purposes to improve the models.
  • Self-assessment (optional) – your own assessment of your Finnish language skills or of your performance in a speaking task, if you choose to provide it.
  • User feedback and opinions (optional) – your opinions about the speaking tasks, the automated speaking assessment results, and the feedback provided by the App, including any free-form comments you choose to provide.

Information Automatically Collected. We automatically log the following when you use the App:

  • A pseudonymous, randomly generated user identifier (no name or email address is stored).
  • Timestamps indicating when app functions are used.
  • Your consent choices relating to participation in the automated speaking assessment research function.

When you use the automated speaking assessment function, we also collect and store:

  • Automated speaking assessment model outputs – the pronunciation and proficiency scores or feedback generated by our Automatic Speech Recognition and automated speaking assessment models from your voice recordings.
  • Your historical performance data – past and current automated speaking assessment ratings associated with your pseudonymous identifier, so you can track your progress over time.
  • Your automated test performance – including your responses and the system’s automated assessment of those responses.

What we do and do not store:

The two functions handle your voice recordings differently:

  • Pronunciation practice function – your voice recordings are processed in real time to generate feedback and are not stored after the result has been returned to you.
  • Automated speaking assessment function – if you choose to use this function, your voice recordings are stored as part of the research dataset, together with the scores and feedback produced by the model. This allows the recordings to be used for improving and evaluating automated speaking assessment models.

For the automated speaking assessment function, we also store the following:

  • Your pseudonymous user identifier.
  • Your consent record for participation in the automated speaking assessment research function.
  • Optional background information if provided.
  • Your self-assessments, if provided.
  • Your opinions about the speaking task, automated speaking assessment results, and feedback, if provided.
  • The practice text you typed or selected.
  • The model output (scores and feedback) for each session.
  • Historical performance records (past and current automated speaking assessment ratings) linked to your pseudonymous identifier.

User Feedback

To help us improve the App and the quality of the automated assessment, we may ask users of the automated speaking assessment function to provide brief in-app feedback. This may include:

  • Overall experience – a simple reaction such as a sad face or smiley face.
  • Assessment accuracy – your view on whether the model measured your speaking performance appropriately.
  • Task opinions – your opinion about the speaking question or task.
  • Free-form comments – an optional text field, if available, where you can share other observations or suggestions.

Providing feedback is entirely voluntary. Responses are stored in association with your pseudonymous user identifier and the relevant exercise session.

Purpose of processing

The personal data described above is collected and processed for the following purposes:

  • To provide the automated speaking assessment and feedback features of the App.
  • To allow you to monitor your own learning progress over time.
  • To develop, train, and evaluate automatic speech recognition and automated speaking assessment models for L2 Finnish as part of the DigiTala in Action research project (Aalto University, University of Helsinki, University of Jyväskylä).
  • To conduct scientific research on second-language speech and speech technology.

We do not use the data to assess or profile you as an individual for any purpose other than providing in-app feedback, and we do not use it for any commercial purpose.

Legal basis

The legal basis for processing is the performance of a task carried out in the public interest (scientific research conducted by Aalto University), in accordance with Article 6(1)(e) of the GDPR and Article 4(3) of the Finnish Data Protection Act. Where you provide optional background information and participate in feedback surveys, we rely on your consent (Article 6(1)(a) GDPR). You may withdraw consent for these optional elements at any time without affecting the core functionality of the App.

Security

We value your trust in providing us your Personal Information, and we strive to use commercially acceptable means of protecting it. Access to stored research data is restricted to authorised members of the research team. However, no method of transmission over the internet or method of electronic storage is 100% secure and reliable, and we cannot guarantee its absolute security.

Therefore, we strongly advise you to review the Privacy Policy of these websites. We have no control over and assume no responsibility for the content, privacy policies, or practices of any third-party sites or services.

Children's Privacy

We do not knowingly collect personally identifiable information from children. We encourage all children to never submit any personally identifiable information through the Application and/or Services. We encourage parents and legal guardians to monitor their children's Internet usage and to help enforce this Policy by instructing their children never to provide personally identifiable information through the Application and/or Services without their permission. If you have reason to believe that a child has provided personally identifiable information to us through the Application and/or Services, please contact us. You must also be at least 16 years of age to consent to the processing of your personally identifiable information in your country (in some countries we may allow your parent or guardian to do so on your behalf).

Your rights

Under the GDPR you have the right to:

  • Know whether your data is being processed.
  • Access your own data.
  • Correct inaccurate or outdated data.
  • Request deletion of your data.
  • Restrict the processing of your data.
  • Object to the processing of your data.
  • Withdraw consent (for optional data elements) at any time.

Because data is stored under a pseudonymous identifier rather than your name, you will need to provide your identifier when exercising these rights. Please note that in some cases research-related obligations may limit the extent to which data can be deleted. You may also stop using the automated speaking assessment function at any time. To exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the details below.

Changes to This Privacy Policy
